The Name "Nile" Comes from the Greek Word "Nelios" which Means "River Valley".

 

The River Nile stretches an astonishing 4184 miles through Egypt and branches off into 8 other African countries. The White Nile flows through Uganda, Sudan and Egypt whilst the Blue Nile starts in Ethiopia. The Blue Nile Branches off into 5 other counties; Zaire, Kenya, Tanzanian, Rwanda, and Burundi each with their own tributaries which flow into into lake Victoria Nyanes or the Nile. The River Nile is most commonly known for its ferocious inhabitants, the crocodiles.  Whereas hundreds of years ago hippos and lions roamed the edges of the Nile but now the dominating force is the largest reptile in the world. It's definitely not recommended go swimming in the Nile as a confrontation with a 1500 lb croc can't guarantee you with all your limbs on the way home. However sightings of these modern day dinosaurs in the Nile are not as frequent as they used to be.
