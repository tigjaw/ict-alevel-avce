Khufu's Great Pyramid:

The largest pyramid ever built, Khufu's Great Pyramid  at Giza consists of approximately 2,300,000 stone blocks weighing up to 15 tonnes each. This pyramid contains 3 burial chambers; the first is underground. The second; the Queen's chamber is found just aboveground. The third is the King's chamber in which a red granite sarcophagus was found exactly in the centre of the Pyramid.

 

 

 

 

Pyramid of Khafre:

Khafre's pyramid can also be found at Giza. As it stands on slightly higher ground it is slightly higher than Khufu's Great Pyramid despite being approximately 10 feet shorter in reality. Khafre's resting place also boasted and unique plethora of statues, including the Sphinx.

 

 

 

 

The Sphinx:

Sitting next to the temples of Khafre the Sphinx's connection with the great Pharaoh is obvious. The face bares a strong resemblance to the man himself whilst the body is of a lion, however it is strangely out of proportion.

The Sphinx was carved out of a stone that already lay in place. Over time the half-man-half-lion statue has naturally eroded with the sand and wind and whilst many attempts have been made to preserve the great structure in modern and ancient times the ware is very apparent. A strong feature is the missing nose, it was rumoured Napoleon's soldiers shot it off but this has since been proven untrue,

 

 

Pyramid of Menkaure:

Standing at 65 metres this pyramid is the smallest pyramid at Giza.  Menkaure built his pyramid 1 tenth the size of Khafre's and instead of using limestone the Pharaoh used the more expensive granite on the bottom levels of the pyramid. in the 19th century excavators found a sarcophagus in Menkaure's burial chamber and shipped it to England only for the boat to sink with the sarcophagus on board.

 

 

 Step Pyramid of Djoser:

 

Djoser's step pyramid began as a normal flat roofed tomb but eventually, over the Pharaoh's 19 year reign had five more layers built atop. At the time of building it was the largest of its kind. The chief architect Imhotep used revolutionary techniques that made this pyramid very durable than its mud-brick counterparts.

Enclosing the step pyramid's complex  was a 10 meter wall which housed temples, courtyards and chapels covering almost 40 acres; the equivalent of the size of a large town in the third millennium B.C.

1,400 years after his death, the achitect Imhotep was exalted to the position of a God, he was also a physician, priest and founder of a cult of healing.

Bent Pyramid:

Initial plans to make the first smooth sided pyramid were modified halfway through construction due to structural instability. This influenced the architects to review the angle of the slant. This was  the first pyramid to have 2 separate burial chambers.

 

 

 

 

 

Red Pyramid:

Also known as the North Pyramid Pharaoh Snefru ordered for its construction soon after the failure of the Bent Pyramid. Snefru may have been buried here after his son had hurried to complete the construction following his father's death.

 

 

 

Maidum Pyramid:

The first ever Egyptian Pyramid with an above-ground burial chamber, the Maidum Pyramid was another production of Pharaoh Snefru. The higher placed tomb is thought to represent the attempt to reach closer to the sun god. Before Snefru died the bottom layers of the pyramid were filled in to form smooth edges such as on a classic pyramid.

 

Today only the highest steps are visible over the deep sands.
