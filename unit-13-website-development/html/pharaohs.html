The title 'Pharaoh' means 'Great House' or palace. He was the most important man in the Kingdom and was thought of as a God. It was believed that a pharaoh could manipulate the weather and his presence could even make the land fertile. To keep the royal blood pure the Pharaoh would often marry a close relative such as a half-sister. The Pharaoh's responsibilities would include being a religious leader, maintaining law and order and even lead the Egyptian army. For every Pharaoh a mastaba or pyramid was built, this would be the resting place for the Pharaoh once he died. Egyptians were strong believers of the after life and if the Pharaoh was not buried in a Pyramid with the correct preparations he would not pass through to the 'next world'. Once a Pharaoh died he became the god Osiris; King of the dead. If the necessary preparations to preserve the dead king's body inside his pyramid were not successful the 'King of the dead' would not survive, the cycle would be broken and Egypt would fall.
