Cairo is the capital City of Egypt.

The "Jewel of the Orient" is the largest city in all middle east and Africa. The population of Cairo or al-Qahirah as it is officially known is 8.1million.

Life in Modern Cairo:

Due to a large part of Egypt's landmass being uninhabitable the places that are tend to be densely populated so space is hard to come by, this is also true of Cairo... The majority of the Cairoene population live in apartments and only the rich can afford to live in a house of their own.

 

At the front of the typical apartment there is a large living room where the family entertains guests. Unrelated guests are not permitted to wonder round the rest of the house.

Formal education is very important in Cairo. Top students can attend the American University in Cairo which teaches courses in English. Cairo has it's own university with over 30,000 students. Many children learn a trade or apprenticeship rather than completing their schooling. Many young Egyptian men migrate to Cairo in search of employment as the City houses the headquarters for most of the country's companies.

 

Every day approximately 1 million people use Cairo's metro system to commute to and from work, mainly factories.

Cairo like other major cities now depends on it's thriving business driven system tradition still leaves a niche for the 'small merchant' selling family crafted items alongside selected imported goods.
