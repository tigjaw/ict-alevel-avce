Egypt really is a desolate land, approximately 95% of the country is barren sands under the scorching sun, but in no way is it lacking personality. The culture and Ancient sites makes this country possibly one of the most exciting places to be. The desert covers the majority of the landscape, the River Nile the only factor that brings life to the area around it which flows the length of the country from south to north. But even in the sandy dunes you can see Egypt for what it really is; it's enchanting history.

The Ancient Pyramids and mysterious Sphinx are just a few of the miraculous artefacts that you'll want to see if you ever travel to Egypt but for now I've supplied the next best thing on the Ancient Egypt pages for you to see the wonders and find out a bit about them.

 

People and Culture:

The majority of the population are Sunni Muslims, but there is a substantial minority of Coptic Christians. Friday is observed as the day of rest. The native language is Arabic but English and French are widely understood.

Most schools are open for 6 hours a day from Saturday to Thursday with only Friday off. Public schooling is free and lasts for 12 years as in Britain.  Top students can attend the American University in Cairo which teaches courses in English. Cairo has it's own university with over 30,000 students. Many children learn a trade or apprenticeship rather than completing their schooling. Many young Egyptian men migrate to Cairo in search of employment as the City houses the headquarters for most of the country's companies.

Traditions:

The birth of a child is cause for a big celebration, following a boy's birth he is circumcised. After the birth of the first son the father and mother take on the titles of "Abu" and "Umm" ("Father" and "Mother") for example if the first son is called "Ali" then the father would be called "Abu Ali". To further celebrate the family slaughters an animal, 2 for boys and 1 for a girl. The family never praise the beauty of the child without giving thanks to God first by saying "Mash'allah".

 

Currency:

The currency is the Egyptian Pound (EGP), 1 Pound equals 100 Piastres or 1000 Milliemes. In comparison to the US dollar, 1 dollar is worth approximately 5.76 EGP and 1 English Pound (£1) = 10.0741 EGP.
