• The pyramids of Egypt are not only the oldest of the seven wonders of the world but the only of those seven wonders to survive to this day.

  • Pyramids were the sacred resting places of the Pharaohs. When the Pharaoh died he would be mummified, placed in a coffin, encased in a sarcophagus and left to rest in the hidden burial chamber. The prayers said for the dead rulers were put on the pyramid walls and later on the coffin.

  • Built around 3000 B.C the Great Pyramid of Cheops consisted of approximately 2,300,000 immense stone blocks weighing a total of 7,000,000 tonnes.

  • Tutankhamun's inner coffin was made of over a tonne of solid gold.

  • Pepi 11 of Egypt always kept several naked slaves nearby whose bodies were smothered in honey, he did this to deter flies from landing on himself.

  • A game similar to bowling was played by ancient Egyptians, they used large stones set up as pins and small stones as a ball.

  • In ancient Egypt priests plucked ever hair from their own bodies.

  • Early Egypt was divided into two Kingdoms; Upper and Lower Egypt.

  • Egyptian writing is called hieroglyphics which is Greek for "Holy writing". There are approximately 700 hieroglyphs. They can be written from left to right, right to left or downwards.

  • The organs of the dead pharaohs were cut out and stored in 'canopy jars' and the body was dried with salt. Only the heart remained in the body.
